Invasive Species in New Zealand

New Zealand is home to a huge number of bird species that evolved there in relative isolation from the rest of the world. Rats, stoats, possum and other invasive species are threatening the native bird populations of New Zealand and the people of the country are in a debate on what to do about it.

The New Zealand government have promised that New Zealand will be predator free by 2050 and are currently discussing methods to tackle this problem.

The purpose of this visualization projecs it to examine the real impact that the invasive rodents are having on the native bird populations and examine what damage has already been done.
